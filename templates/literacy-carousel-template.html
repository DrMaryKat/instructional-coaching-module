Literacy Concept Carousel

Professional Development Station Materials

Building Foundational Literacy Knowledge for Middle School Teachers

Station 1: Phoneme-Grapheme Mapping

15 minutes

Materials Needed:

  • Phoneme cards - Individual sound cards (/b/, /ch/, /oi/, etc.)
  • Grapheme tiles - Letter and letter combination tiles (b, ch, oy, etc.)
  • Word lists - Grade-appropriate words for mapping practice
  • Mapping charts - Blank phoneme-grapheme correspondence charts
  • Colored markers - For highlighting patterns
  • Sticky notes - For participant observations

Station Activities:

  1. Introduction (3 minutes)
    • Define phoneme: smallest unit of sound in language
    • Define grapheme: letter(s) that represent a phoneme
    • Show example: word "chat" has 3 phonemes (/ch/ /a/ /t/) and 4 letters
  2. Hands-On Mapping (7 minutes)
    • Participants select 5 words from the word list
    • Break each word into phonemes using sound cards
    • Match each phoneme to its grapheme representation
    • Record mappings on chart
  3. Pattern Recognition (3 minutes)
    • Identify words with more letters than sounds
    • Find examples of digraphs (ch, sh, th)
    • Locate silent letters
  4. Application (2 minutes)
    • Share one insight about teaching implications
    • Write one question on a sticky note

Example Mapping:

Word Phonemes Graphemes Notes
night /n/ /ī/ /t/ n - igh - t "igh" = 3 letters for 1 sound
phone /f/ /ō/ /n/ ph - o - ne "ph" = digraph, silent "e"

Facilitator Notes:

  • Emphasize that English has 44 phonemes but only 26 letters
  • This mismatch is why phoneme-grapheme knowledge is crucial
  • Connect to student struggles with decoding
  • Be prepared for questions about regional pronunciation differences

Station 2: Morphology in Action

15 minutes

Materials Needed:

  • Root word cards - Common Latin and Greek roots (bio, graph, port, etc.)
  • Prefix cards - Common prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, dis-, etc.)
  • Suffix cards - Common suffixes (-tion, -ment, -ful, -less, etc.)
  • Word building mats - Template for building words
  • Meaning charts - Reference sheets with morpheme meanings
  • Academic vocabulary list - Grade 4-8 vocabulary words

Station Activities:

  1. Morpheme Introduction (3 minutes)
    • Define morpheme: smallest unit of meaning
    • Distinguish between free morphemes (can stand alone) and bound morphemes (must attach)
    • Show how one root can create multiple words
  2. Word Building Challenge (7 minutes)
    • Teams select one root word card
    • Build as many real words as possible using prefixes/suffixes
    • Record words and their meanings
    • Example: "port" → transport, portable, import, export, porter
  3. Meaning Analysis (3 minutes)
    • Break down 2-3 academic vocabulary words
    • Show how knowing morphemes helps decode meaning
    • Example: "photosynthesis" = photo (light) + synthesis (putting together)
  4. Teaching Application (2 minutes)
    • Discuss how to teach morphology to struggling readers
    • Share one strategy for your grade level

Morpheme Word Web Example:

                         biography
                            |
                    auto----|----graphy
                            |
                         GRAPH
                      (to write)
                            |
                    photo---|----ic
                            |
                        paragraph

Facilitator Notes:

  • Emphasize that morphology instruction significantly improves vocabulary
  • Connect to content area reading (science and social studies vocabulary)
  • Highlight that 60% of academic words have Latin or Greek origins
  • Prepare examples relevant to participants' subject areas

Station 3: Fluency Building Strategies

15 minutes

Materials Needed:

  • Grade-level passage samples - Various genres and complexity levels
  • Fluency rubrics - Assessment tools for rate, accuracy, expression
  • Timer/stopwatch - For timed readings
  • Phrase-cued text samples - Text marked for phrase boundaries
  • Audio recordings - Examples of fluent reading
  • Fluency tracking charts - Progress monitoring tools

Station Activities:

  1. Fluency Components Review (3 minutes)
    • Rate: appropriate speed
    • Accuracy: correct word recognition
    • Expression: prosody and phrasing
    • Comprehension: understanding while reading
  2. Strategy Demonstration (5 minutes)
    • Model echo reading with a passage
    • Demonstrate phrase-cued reading
    • Show repeated reading technique
    • Practice reader's theater approach
  3. Partner Practice (5 minutes)
    • Pairs practice one fluency strategy
    • Use fluency rubric to provide feedback
    • Switch roles and repeat
  4. Adaptation Discussion (2 minutes)
    • How to adapt for struggling readers
    • Technology tools for fluency practice

Phrase-Cued Text Example:

The old lighthouse / stood proudly / on the rocky cliff, //
its beacon sweeping / across the dark ocean / every night. //
For generations, / it had guided ships / safely to harbor, //
a silent guardian / watching over / the treacherous waters. //

Key: / = short pause, // = longer pause

Fluency Strategy Best For Implementation
Echo Reading Building confidence Teacher reads, student echoes
Choral Reading Whole class engagement Everyone reads together
Partner Reading Peer support Students alternate paragraphs
Repeated Reading Increasing rate Same passage 3-4 times

Facilitator Notes:

  • Stress that fluency is not just speed - expression matters
  • Connect fluency to comprehension (can't understand if struggling to decode)
  • Provide grade-specific WCPM (words correct per minute) targets
  • Address the needs of older struggling readers (age-appropriate texts)

Station 4: Comprehension Scaffolds

15 minutes

Materials Needed:

  • Strategy posters - Visual reminders of comprehension strategies
  • Graphic organizers - Various types for different text structures
  • Text samples - Fiction and nonfiction passages
  • Question stems - Differentiated by DOK levels
  • Think-aloud scripts - Examples of metacognitive modeling
  • Sticky notes - For annotation practice

Station Activities:

  1. Strategy Overview (3 minutes)
    • Before Reading: Preview, predict, activate prior knowledge
    • During Reading: Monitor, visualize, question, clarify
    • After Reading: Summarize, synthesize, evaluate
  2. Graphic Organizer Match (5 minutes)
    • Match text structures to appropriate organizers
    • Cause/Effect → Flow chart
    • Compare/Contrast → Venn diagram
    • Sequence → Timeline
    • Problem/Solution → T-chart
  3. Think-Aloud Practice (5 minutes)
    • Model metacognitive thinking with a passage
    • Partners practice with new text
    • Focus on making thinking visible
  4. Scaffold Selection (2 minutes)
    • Choose appropriate scaffolds for struggling readers
    • Discuss gradual release of responsibility

Comprehension Strategy Bookmark:

BEFORE:
• What do I already know?
• What do I predict?
• What's my purpose?

DURING:
• Do I understand?
• What's confusing?
• What's important?

AFTER:
• What did I learn?
• What questions remain?
• How does this connect?

Reader Profile Recommended Scaffolds
Struggling with vocabulary Word walls, context clue charts, morphology focus
Poor working memory Graphic organizers, chunked text, visual supports
Lacks background knowledge Pre-reading activities, video clips, picture walks
Difficulty with inference Think-alouds, question frames, evidence charts

Facilitator Notes:

  • Emphasize explicit strategy instruction, not just practice
  • Connect to content area reading requirements
  • Discuss how to differentiate scaffolds without stigmatizing
  • Provide examples from actual middle school texts
